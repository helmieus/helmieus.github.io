# Hello world!

Okay, so… I finally made a blog.  
Yeah, it’s kinda late—I’m already in my final year—but better late than never, right? 😅

Honestly, I’ve been thinking about this for a while. Not because I want to be “famous” or anything (hehe), but more like… **I need a place to dump all the stuff I learn**. Especially now that I’m diving deep into red teaming and CTFs.

So yeah, here I’ll probably post:
- My CTF write-ups (mostly TryHackMe & HTB)
- Random posts on my journey
- Stuff I break (and sometimes fix)
- And maybe some thoughts while waiting for my internship to start

---

### How I got into this mess called “hacking”

It all started during matriculation—thanks to a **TikTok video** (yes, really 😂). The video talked about TryHackMe platform, and I was like, *“Wahhh,, ada platform rupanya.”*

So I signed up.  
And I haven’t stopped since.

Now, I’m a Network Engineering student who’s way too obsessed with finding ways to break into things (legally, of course 👀). And after internship? Nahh, I'm thinking to travel around the world.

We’ll see.

For now, this blog is just my little corner to **learn in public**. No pressure, no fancy words—just me figuring things out.

Oh, and by the way—I have **Cerebral Palsy (CP)**.  
But it’s not the “dramatic” kind. I can walk, run, type fast, and do pretty much everything a “normal” person can do—just at my own pace.  

I might not be built for martial arts or sprinting races…  
**But online? Everything’s fair game.**  
Firewalls don’t care how I walk. Machines don’t judge my gait. They only care if you know the right command—and I’m learning those every day.

So yeah, even me—a guy with CP—can hack.  
Not despite it. Just… *with it*. Like any other part of me.
